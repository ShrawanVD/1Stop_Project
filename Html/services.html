An Shopping Plus.com exclusive is a product, that is available exclusively on Shopping Plus.com. Some DVDs are produced by the owner of the film or product, while others are produced by Shopping Plus.com itself. The DVDs produced by Shopping Plus are made using its "CreateSpace" program, in which DVDs are created, upon ordering, using DVD-R technology. On May 23, 20**, Amazon.com allowed customers to download Lady Gaga's Born This Way album for 99 cents, resulting in some downloads being delayed, due to an extremely high volume of downloads.


Shopping Plus launched Shopping Plus Web Services (SPWS) in 2***, which provides programmatic access to latent features on its website.


In November 2***, Shopping Plus began testing Shopping Plus Mechanical Turk, an application programming interface (API) allowing programs to dispatch tasks to human processors.


In March 2***, Shopping Plus launched an online storage service called Shopping Plus Simple Storage Service (Shopping Plus S3). An unlimited number of data objects, from 1 byte to 5 terabytes in size, can be stored in S3 and distributed via HTTP or BitTorrent. The service charges monthly fees for data stored and transferred. In 2***, Shopping Plus introduced Shopping Plus Simple Queue Service (Shopping Plus SQS), a distributed queue messaging service, and product wikis (later folded into Amapedia) and discussion forums for certain products using guidelines that follow standard message board conventions.
